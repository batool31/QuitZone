The Health Risks

Smoking is the leading cause of preventable diseases, including lung cancer, heart disease, and respiratory disorders. It damages nearly every organ in your body.

  • Increases risk of cardiovascular diseases
  • Causes COPD and emphysema
  • Linked to multiple types of cancer
  • Weakens immune system function
  • Reduces fertility in both men and women

Every cigarette contains over 7,000 chemicals, at least 70 of which are known to cause cancer. Your body begins to heal within minutes of quitting.

Environmental Impact

Not only does smoking harm your health, but it also damages the environment. Cigarette production, consumption, and disposal all contribute to pollution.

  • Cigarette butts are the most littered item
  • Toxic chemicals leach into soil and water
  • Takes up to 10 years to decompose
  • Deforestation for tobacco farming
  • Produces 84 million tons of CO2 yearly

By quitting smoking, you're not only improving your health but also making a positive impact on the environment around you.

The Financial Cost

Smoking is expensive. The money spent on cigarettes could be put to better use, like saving for your future or investing in healthier habits.

  • Average smoker spends $2,300+ yearly
  • Medical costs can exceed $35,000 per year
  • Higher insurance premiums
  • Reduced home and car resale value
  • Lost productivity and sick days
